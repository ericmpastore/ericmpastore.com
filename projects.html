Uncovering Emerging Trends in Information Technology: An Industry Survey (M.S. Capstone Project)

Modern life depends on good tech. Good tech depends on good software.

Problem Description

Every day billions of users fire up PCs, tablets, smartphones, and a thousand other devices for work, play, and everything in between. It takes many more apps, scripts, and pages to enable these devices to serve human needs. Whether we understand it or not, software drives us.

Many times, software is the problem. Even assuming there are no bugs the average user still needs to understand the application. And even if the user can understand, the developer has to understand how to maintain the code. Bad code (buggy, unclear, and full of hacks) is the problem. Good code (efficient, intuitive, and standard) is the solution. We go to the experts to find the solution.

The experts are software engineers. These are the designers and architects of code, who take business requirements and design the interfaces and parts that make up an application.

Methodology

The goal of this project was to identify issues in software development through interviews with professional software engineers. The engineers were asked about the issues they see in the industry through the following questions:

  • In your opinion, what is the most critical issue facing the software industry at large? Please explain.
  • What is the most critical issue facing your organization? Please explain.
  • Which types of issues are the most difficult to solve: purely technical issues or those involving human factors? Why?
  • Other questions, as described in the Proposal.
